About me

I'm Zerah, a Senior UX Researcher with 8+ years' experience helping teams build with users' needs at the forefront. I've led research across global markets at Block (Afterpay and Cash App), Mastercard, and Tabcorp, working closely with Product and Design to translate complex human behaviour into clear product direction.

I bring a rigorous yet efficient approach to research, moving quickly without cutting corners and delivering insights that are ready to act on. I'm also deeply curious about where AI is best placed to sharpen and extend my craft.

I care about understanding people. Not just what they say, but how they actually behave. My research is grounded in the belief that building for real user needs is the most sustainable path to business success, and the best defence against products gradually worsening for the people they're meant to serve.

Selected work

The work

Due to confidentiality, I keep project details private, but I'm always happy to talk through my experience in conversation. Below is a snapshot of the types of research I've led across global teams. Ask me about any of these.

01
Foundational Discovery
Understanding users at the root
I work best at the fuzzy front end, when the problem space is ambiguous and the team needs clear direction before they can move. My exploratory work is as much about finding the right frame as it is finding the right method.
  • Mapped behavioural analogies from fitness and habit-change apps to unlock design direction for a sustainability feature; a reframe that made a complex problem suddenly tractable
  • Designed focus group activities (brand personas, empathy profiles, magic wand exercises) that surfaced unspoken mental models in ways a discussion guide alone couldn't
  • Led foundational research to define customer cohorts and unmet needs ahead of a major strategic initiative; work later credited with putting the team "on the right track" for annual planning
  • Explored how teens think about money management, and how group spend dynamics shape financial decision-making across social and family contexts
In-depth interviews Focus groups Mental models Global markets
02
Evaluative & Usability Research
Validating decisions with real users
I bring a clear sense of what actually needs answering at each stage, and the discipline to not over-engineer it. Good evaluative research gives teams confidence in the decisions that matter, and clarity on what to fix.
  • Led sequential rounds of usability testing on an industry-first AR product, progressively sharpening comprehension, findability and experience quality before launch
  • Ran rapid usability studies across multiple concurrent product workstreams, delivering prioritised, bite-sized recommendations that shaped design decisions in real time
  • Managed and analysed 60+ unmoderated interviews in a single week by integrating AI tooling into the synthesis workflow, without compromising on quality or rigour
  • Tested everything from onboarding journeys and reward structures to concept variants and in-product copy across fintech and wagering contexts
Usability testing Concept testing Unmoderated research AI-assisted synthesis
03
Synthesis & Insight Communication
Making research land across teams
Research that stays in a deck doesn't move anything. I put real craft into the moments where insight meets the people who need to act on it.
  • Designed and facilitated iterative ideation workshops across hemispheres, pivoting the format mid-sprint when the structure wasn't serving the team
  • Led "How Might We" sessions that took raw findings and turned them into concrete design direction, keeping teams focused without narrowing the solution space too early
  • Ran prioritisation workshops helping cross-functional teams identify the highest-impact, lowest-effort fixes from a landscape of research findings
  • Facilitated a planning session called out by senior stakeholders as "structured, inclusive, and energising", one that directly shaped the product roadmap
Insight synthesis Stakeholder workshops How Might We Storytelling
